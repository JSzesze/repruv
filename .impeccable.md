## Design Context

### Users

People who need clean, portable Markdown from a public webpage or X post without creating an account. The core audience includes writers, researchers, developers, and people feeding web material into notes or AI workflows. They arrive with a URL and want a trustworthy result immediately.

### Brand Personality

Precise, ruthless, immediate. Repruv should feel like a sharp public instrument: confident enough to be memorable, quiet enough to stay out of the user's way, and candid about caching and public URLs.

### Aesthetic Direction

Brutally minimal and industrial, using Factory.ai as a quality reference rather than a template. Favor a warm near-black canvas, hard white typography, signal-orange state accents, compact operational language, thin rules, and a conversion surface that is the hero. Avoid decorative SaaS sections, glass effects, generic card grids, neon gradients, and ornamental AI imagery. The primary experience should work equally well on desktop and mobile, with restrained motion and strong keyboard/focus behavior.

### Design Principles

1. The URL is the only required input; make the path from paste to Markdown feel instantaneous.
2. Treat system state as part of the visual identity: ready, processing, cached, copied, and failed should be unmistakable.
3. Earn every element. If it does not help users convert, inspect, copy, download, or call the API, remove it.
4. Use scale, spacing, rules, and typography for impact; decoration remains rare and functional.
5. Keep the public-service contract visible: free, anonymous, cached, and intended only for public URLs.
